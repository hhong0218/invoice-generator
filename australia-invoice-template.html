Australia Invoice Template

Australian invoicing has clear ATO rules — particularly around the ABN, GST, and what makes a document a valid “tax invoice.” This guide explains them and lets you create a compliant Australian invoice free in your browser.

Tax invoice requirements

If you’re registered for GST, the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) requires a valid tax invoice for sales over a set amount so your customer can claim a GST credit. A compliant tax invoice shows:

  • The words “Tax invoice”, clearly stated.
  • Your business or trading name and your ABN (Australian Business Number).
  • The invoice date.
  • A description of the items sold, including quantity and price.
  • The GST amount (or a statement that the total includes GST), shown so the 10% component is identifiable.
  • For invoices over the ATO threshold, the buyer’s identity or ABN.

GST in Australia

GST is a flat 10% on most goods and services. You must register for GST once your turnover reaches the ATO threshold (and may register voluntarily below it). Registered businesses add 10% GST to taxable sales, claim GST credits on business purchases, and report via Business Activity Statements (BAS). Some supplies are GST-free (such as many basic foods and certain health and education services) or input-taxed — if you sell these, your invoice should make the GST treatment clear rather than adding 10% by default.

The ABN and why it matters

Your ABN is central to Australian invoicing. If you don’t quote an ABN on an invoice, the paying business may be required to withhold tax (currently the top rate) from your payment under the “no-ABN withholding” rule — so always include it. If you’re not registered for GST, you still issue a regular invoice (not a “tax invoice”) with your ABN, and you simply don’t add GST.

Payment terms and methods

Common Australian terms are 7, 14, or 30 days from the invoice date. Bank transfer (EFT) using BSB and account number is the standard payment method; PayID, BPAY, cards, and payment links are also widely used. State a clear due date, your BSB and account details (or a payment link), and any late-payment terms in the invoice notes so the customer can pay without follow-up.

Frequently asked questions

What makes a valid tax invoice in Australia?

For GST-registered sellers, a tax invoice must state “Tax invoice”, show your name and ABN, the date, a description of the items with quantity and price, and the GST amount or a statement that the price includes GST. Larger invoices also need the buyer’s identity or ABN.

Do I have to include my ABN?

Yes — always. If you don’t quote an ABN, the paying business may have to withhold tax from your payment under the no-ABN withholding rule. Including your ABN avoids this whether or not you’re registered for GST.

When do I charge GST?

Once you’re registered for GST, you add 10% to taxable sales. Until you reach the registration threshold (or register voluntarily) you don’t charge GST and issue a regular invoice rather than a tax invoice. Some supplies are GST-free or input-taxed.

What payment terms are common in Australia?

Terms of 7, 14, or 30 days are typical. EFT via BSB and account number is standard, with PayID, BPAY, cards, and payment links also common. State a clear due date and your payment details on the invoice.
