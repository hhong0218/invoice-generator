Tutor Invoice Template

Whether you teach math, music, or a language, tutoring invoices need to be simple and clear — often sent to a parent rather than the student. This guide covers how to bill per session, per hour, or by package, and lets you generate a professional invoice free in your browser.

How tutors usually bill

Tutoring billing is refreshingly straightforward, and most tutors use one of three approaches:

  • Per session. A flat price for each lesson of a fixed length — the easiest model for weekly students.
  • Per hour. Useful when session lengths vary. Put your hourly rate in the unit price and the hours taught in the quantity column.
  • Lesson packages. A bundle — for example ten sessions paid in advance — often at a small discount. Bill the package as one line and note how many sessions it covers.

Because many tutors invoice a parent or guardian, address the invoice to the bill-payer while noting the student’s name in the line items or notes, so it’s clear which child the lessons were for.

Billing in advance vs. in arrears

Tutors split between billing at the start of a period and at the end. In advance — invoicing for the coming month or a package before lessons begin — protects your time and reduces no-shows, since the parent has already committed. In arrears — invoicing for sessions already taught — is simpler when schedules are irregular. Either works; just be consistent and state on the invoice which sessions the charge covers, with their dates.

Cancellations and no-shows

A clear cancellation policy avoids awkward conversations. Many tutors require 24 hours’ notice and charge for late cancellations or no-shows. State the policy in your invoice notes or terms so a charged missed session is never a surprise. If you bill in advance for a package, note whether unused sessions roll over or expire.

Recurring monthly invoices

For ongoing students, a monthly invoice listing each session and its date is the cleanest record — it doubles as an attendance summary the parent can keep. Reuse the same invoice number scheme (for example a student initial plus a sequence) so your records stay tidy across the year. Issue on the same day each month so families can budget for it.

Keep it simple and professional

Tutoring is built on trust, and a clear invoice reinforces it. Make sure yours has a unique number, the issue date, your name and the parent’s or student’s details, itemized sessions with dates, the subtotal, any tax, and the total due. Frequently asked questions

Should I bill tutoring in advance or after lessons?

Both are common. Billing in advance — for a month or a package — protects your time and cuts no-shows, while billing in arrears suits irregular schedules. Pick one, be consistent, and list the covered session dates on the invoice.

How do I handle a parent paying for a student?

Address the invoice to the parent or guardian as the bill-payer, and note the student’s name in the line items or notes so it’s clear which child the lessons were for.

Should I charge for missed lessons?

Many tutors require 24 hours’ notice and bill for late cancellations or no-shows. State the policy in your invoice notes so a charged missed session is never a surprise.

Do tutors need to charge tax?

Most independent tutors don’t, but it depends on your location and whether educational services are taxable there. Check your local rules and add a tax line on the invoice only if required.
