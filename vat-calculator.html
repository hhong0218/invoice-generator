What is this VAT calculator?

This tool calculates Value Added Tax (VAT) or Goods and Services Tax (GST) using each country's standard rate — the default rate that applies to most goods and services. Enter an amount and pick a country to add VAT and see the total, or switch to reverse mode to enter a VAT-inclusive total and see the VAT portion and pre-tax amount broken out separately.

How it's calculated

Adding VAT: VAT = Amount × Rate, and Total = Amount + VAT.

Removing VAT (reverse): Net amount = Total ÷ (1 + Rate), and VAT = Total − Net amount. Dividing by (1 + rate) is the correct way to "un-add" VAT — simply subtracting the percentage from the total would understate the net amount and misstate the VAT portion.

Notes on specific countries

  • Canada: the federal Goods and Services Tax (GST) is 5% nationwide, but most provinces add their own provincial sales tax or a combined Harmonized Sales Tax (HST) on top — combined rates range roughly from 5% (Alberta, no PST) up to 15% (several Atlantic provinces). This calculator shows the federal GST rate only; check your specific province for the real combined rate.
  • India: GST uses a multi-slab system rather than one flat rate. Following India's GST 2.0 reform (effective September 2025), most goods and services fall into a simplified 5% or 18% structure, with a 40% "de-merit" rate reserved for luxury and sin goods. This calculator uses 18% as the standard/most common rate — always confirm the slab for your specific product or service.
  • EU average: shown for reference — individual EU member states set their own standard rate (minimum 15% under EU rules), so always use the specific country's rate for real transactions.

Frequently asked questions

Does this show the exact VAT I'll be charged?

It shows each country's standard rate, which applies to most goods and services. Many countries also have reduced rates (often for food, books, or children's products) or exemptions, so the exact rate for a specific purchase can differ from the standard rate shown here.

How do I remove VAT from a total?

Divide the VAT-inclusive total by (1 + the VAT rate as a decimal). For example, to remove 20% VAT from a £120 total: 120 ÷ 1.20 = £100 net, so the VAT portion is £20. Switch this calculator to "Remove VAT" mode to do this automatically.

What's the difference between VAT and GST?

They're functionally the same idea — a consumption tax collected at each stage of the supply chain and ultimately paid by the end consumer — just with different names in different countries. Australia, New Zealand, Canada, Singapore, and India call it GST; the UK, EU countries, and most others call it VAT.

Why does Canada show only 5%?

5% is the federal GST that applies everywhere in Canada. Most provinces add their own provincial sales tax or combine it into an HST, so the real combined rate in a given province is usually higher than 5% — check your specific province for the exact combined rate.

Is my data uploaded anywhere?

No. This calculator runs entirely in your browser using plain JavaScript. Nothing you enter is sent to a server or stored.
