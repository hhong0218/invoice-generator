What is this sales tax calculator?

This tool estimates US sales tax using each state's state-level base rate — the tax set by the state government itself. Pick a state, enter a subtotal, and the calculator multiplies it by that state's rate to show the tax amount and the total you'd pay. Switch to reverse mode to do the opposite: enter a total that already includes tax, and the calculator divides out the tax to show what the pre-tax subtotal must have been.

How it's calculated

Forward mode: Tax = Subtotal × State rate, and Total = Subtotal + Tax.

Reverse mode: Subtotal = Total ÷ (1 + State rate), and Tax = Total − Subtotal. This correctly "un-does" the tax rather than just subtracting a percentage of the total, which would overstate the tax removed.

Important: this is a state base rate, not your local rate

Actual sales tax almost always differs from the state base rate. Most states allow counties, cities, and special districts to add their own local sales tax on top of the state rate. Combined state + local rates can run several percentage points higher than the state rate alone — in some high-tax jurisdictions the combined rate exceeds 10%, even when the state base rate is much lower. This calculator gives you a quick starting estimate using the state-level rate only. For an exact rate, check your specific city/county using your state's department of revenue lookup tool or a point-of-sale tax service, especially before quoting a customer or filing a return.

Frequently asked questions

Is this the exact sales tax I'll pay at checkout?

Not necessarily. This calculator uses each state's base rate only. Most states let counties and cities add local sales tax on top, so your actual checkout rate is often higher than what's shown here. Use this as a quick estimate, then confirm the exact local rate for a specific address if precision matters (e.g., for invoicing or tax filing).

Why do some states show 0%?

Alaska, Delaware, Montana, New Hampshire, and Oregon have no state-level sales tax. Alaska does allow some local jurisdictions to levy their own local sales tax even though the state rate is 0%, so a small local tax can still apply there.

How does the reverse calculation work?

If you only know a tax-inclusive total (for example, a total you already charged a customer), the calculator divides by (1 + rate) rather than just subtracting a flat percentage. This correctly isolates the original pre-tax subtotal and the exact tax dollar amount, avoiding a common rounding mistake.

Is my data uploaded anywhere?

No. This calculator runs entirely in your browser using plain JavaScript. Nothing you enter is sent to a server, stored, or tracked beyond standard anonymous analytics.

Is this tax advice?

No. This tool provides a general estimate for planning and quoting purposes only. Sales tax rules (what's taxable, exemptions, nexus, filing frequency) vary by state and by product or service type. Consult your state's department of revenue or a tax professional for compliance-grade numbers.
