How to invoice as a freelancer

If you're self-employed, sending a clear invoice is how you get paid. With this free freelance invoice generator it takes three steps:

  • Add your details. Enter your name or business name and contact info under From, then your client's details under Bill to. Set an invoice number, the issue date, a due date, and the currency you bill in.
  • List the work you did. Add a line for each service, project, or block of hours with a description, quantity (hours or units), and your rate. Line totals, the subtotal, tax, and grand total are calculated automatically — handy whether you charge hourly or by fixed project fee.
  • Download or print. Click Download PDF to save a clean, professional invoice you can email to your client, or use Print. Upload your logo first if you want to brand it.

What to include on a freelance invoice

A professional freelance invoice helps you look credible and avoid back-and-forth. Make sure yours covers:

  • The word "Invoice" and a unique invoice number so both you and your client can reference it later.
  • Your name or business name and contact details, plus the client's billing details (including any company or project name they ask you to address it to).
  • The issue date and a clear payment due date so there's no ambiguity about when payment is expected.
  • An itemized breakdown of your services — hours and hourly rate, or a fixed fee per deliverable.
  • Subtotal, any tax you're required to charge, and the total amount due in your chosen currency.
  • Payment terms and instructions in the notes — accepted payment methods, your bank or payment details, and any late-payment policy.

Tips for getting paid faster

A few small habits make a big difference to your cash flow as a freelancer:

  • Send the invoice promptly. Bill as soon as the work is delivered rather than waiting until month-end — the sooner it lands, the sooner it gets paid.
  • State clear payment terms. Spell out the due date and your preferred payment method up front so the client knows exactly what to do.
  • Be specific in descriptions. Clear line items that match what you agreed on reduce questions and approval delays.
  • Use consistent invoice numbers. Sequential numbers keep your own records tidy and make it easy to follow up on anything unpaid.
  • Keep a copy. Save the PDF for your records so you can track what's outstanding and have it ready at tax time.
